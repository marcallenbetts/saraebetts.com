Work Values Inventory

Psychologist and career theorist Donald J. Super’s work was concerned primarily with the development of a person’s self-concept as it relates to their career choices. The values you have toward careers and work, he said, will impact how you make decisions about which jobs to pursue, as well as why you leave jobs. Super also believed that we all go through various stages of development throughout our lives in which we prioritize different things. Your values are important to consider when making educational and career choices, because it is stressful to work at a place that is not consistent with what is personally important to you. This assignment will help you identify the values that are important to you in a job/career and help your Career Navigators when they are assisting you with employment opportunities.

Understand that these values may change and shift over time and depending on circumstance, and you may want/need to revisit them on occasion to gain a better understanding of what is important to you in the job search. Please feel free to reach out to your Career Navigator to obtain a new copy of this form when needed.

Value Descriptions

  • Creativity: Work that permits one to invent new things, design new products, or develop new ideas.
  • Management: Work that permits one to plan and lay out work for others.
  • Achievement: Work that gives one the feeling of accomplishment.
  • Surroundings: Work that is carried out in pleasant conditions.
  • Supervisory Relationships: Work that is carried out under a supervisor who is fair, supportive, and with whom you get along.
  • Way of Life: Work that permits one to live the kind of life he/she chooses and to be the type of person he/she wishes to be.
  • Security: Work that provides one with the certainty of having a job, even in difficult economic times.
  • Co-Workers: Work that connects one with fellow workers who are liked.
  • Aesthetic: Work that permits one to be surrounded by beautiful things and contribute beauty to the world.
  • Prestige: Work that gives one standing in the eyes of others.

Instructions

Rank each statement using the drop-down menu; a “1” means “unimportant,” while a “5” means “very important.” For example, the first statement is, “Have to keep solving problems.” If this is something you value highly in your work, you would rank it as a “5.” After you rank all of the statements, you will see a series of values with your score next to each. The higher your score for a value, the more important it is to you in your work.
